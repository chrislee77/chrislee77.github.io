Benefits of Hiking

Online Research

1. Hiking keeps your mind more focused and sharper than many other forms of exercise

- Research shows that hiking doesn't just feel good, but it also keeps your brain mentally healthy

- Although all exercise is good for you, hiking involves something many other exercises don't. Trails.

- Simply, this means navigating in a world that isn't completely predictable with hidden obstacles along the way

- Examples could be: dirt, overhanging branches, hidden obstacles, trail markers, animals etc.

- All of these different obstacles you face keeps you on your toes and require micro/macro adjustments which is great for your brain

2. It helps you to keep calm and peaceful

- Exercise in general can be great at releasing stress, but hiking in particular is especially good at this.

- Hiking can be set apart from other forms of exercise since it's done outdoors and in a natural setting

- Now, you may be thinking 'Aren't there other activities that are also done outdoors?' and you would be correct in thinking that

- Examples include: river rafting, backpacking etc. However, activities like this require much more planning compared to a simple hike

- Hiking can happen anywhere, and you still get to experience the beautiful surrounding nature

- Studies have found that walking in greenery often can help us heal from what is known as an "Attention Overload"

- The mental fatigue that comes from living in a world full of computers is a constant distraction

- Being in nature is also very calming too. In fact, studies have found that people who spend more time walking in nature are less anxious

- This can help protect from depression

3. It helps your relationships

- There is lots of evidence showing that hiking improves relationships

- One reason that many people hike with others is for a feeling of closeness or safety

- Having a friend along the way can be a great way to connect with them free of distractions

- Further research shows that exposure to nature can help our relationships by making us more empathetic, helpful, and generous

- Even when hiking by yourself, it can help to reduce stress and make you feel good in general

- These effects can also help your relationships in that they reflect in your interactions once returning from the hike

4. Increases your creativity

- There was a test where young adults spent three days hiking in the wilderness

- The participants showed increased creative thinking and problem-solving after the experience

- Other studies have found connections between creative thinking and experiences in nature

- Some scholars believe that these benefits for creativity have to do with how natural settings allow our attention to soften

- It also allows our minds to wander in ways that can help us connect ideas that are swirling around in our minds

5. Helps create a positive relationship with nature

- Although hiking is extremely beneficial for us, what you may not notice is that it can also be beneficial to the environment itself

- Think about it, if we have the stamina to cover long distances, we could all use cars less and reduce all of our carbon footprints

- Hiking can also benefit our planet in another way

- For example, developing a positive relationship with nature can cause us to care more about it

- As a result of this, we begin to care more about its fate and make us consider conservation methods

- I think this shows how hiking may be one of the most effective and meaningful forms of transport, being beneficial to both the environment and ourselves
